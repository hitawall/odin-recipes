Kadhi Chawal

Description

Kadhi chawal is a popular dish in Indian cuisine that consists of a lentil and yogurt-based curry called kadhi, served with rice. The kadhi is made by simmering yogurt, gram flour, and spices in water until it thickens into a creamy consistency. The rice is typically cooked separately and then served with the kadhi on top. Kadhi chawal is often garnished with fried onions and served with pickles or other accompaniments on the side. It is a delicious and satisfying meal that is enjoyed by many people in India and around the world.

Ingredients

  • Yogurt or curd
  • Gram flour or chickpea flour
  • Water
  • Salt
  • Turmeric powder
  • Red chili powder
  • Cumin seeds
  • Mustard seeds
  • Asafoetida
  • Dried fenugreek leaves
  • Green chili peppers
  • Ginger
  • Garlic
  • Lemon juice
  • Rice
  • Fried onions for garnishing

Steps

  1. In a mixing bowl, whisk together the yogurt, gram flour, and a few cups of water until smooth.
  2. In a large saucepan, heat some oil over medium heat. Add the cumin seeds, mustard seeds, and asafoetida, and let them sizzle for a few seconds.
  3. Pour the yogurt mixture into the saucepan and stir to combine. Add the turmeric, red chili powder, salt, and dried fenugreek leaves, and stir to incorporate.
  4. Bring the kadhi to a gentle boil, then reduce the heat to low and let it simmer for 20-30 minutes, stirring occasionally, until it has thickened to a creamy consistency.
  5. While the kadhi is simmering, cook the rice according to the package instructions. You can use white rice, brown rice, or another type of rice, depending on your preference.
  6. Once the kadhi and rice are cooked, transfer the rice to a serving bowl and spoon the kadhi on top. Garnish with fried onions and serve with pickles or other accompaniments on the side.
